			Story

In the game you play the role of Detective Prescott. A private investigator who has always had a strange fascination and suspicion over the people who have gone missing at the local Fredbear's Diner & Arcade.

One night you get a strange call in your office from a distressed sounding mother, who claims her 8 year old daughter Lily, did not return home from a birthday party she had attended early at the diner.

The mother desperate to find her daughter pleads for you to go search for her, or any signs of her inside the diner.

Eager to help the poor woman, -and to have a chance to explore the diner yourself, you accept her offer.

Upon sneaking into the establishment and having a look around, you slowly begin to realize something is amiss: Strange notes, newspaper clippings -oh and homicidal animatronics.

		
			Information

Missing at Fredbear's is a Five Nights at Freddy's fan game which pulls elements from the original FNaF by

@realscawthon
, and puts a 3D free-roam twist on it.

Taking place of course in Fredbear's Diner, you must journey into the dark establishment at night completing objectives/minigames, finding secrets, all while surviving a hoard deadly animatronics both familiar and new...

		
			Q & A

  • When and will the game be released?

    A: Unlike many Fredbear games (or just fangames in general), I understand much disappointment stems from the fact that they are usually cancelled either through releases or before they are even released.

    Unless in the case of serious corruption, this game will not be cancelled for any other reason.

    As for an actual release date, the goal is to have the game out by late February to early March, but depending on potential obstacles this may change at any time.

  • Will the game be ported to mobile/other platforms?

    A: Not for mobile, but there may be a chance of it being ported to other PC platforms such as Mac. This is not guaranteed.

  • Is this just another TNaF remake?

    A: Not necessarily, it's really more of a reimagining that takes some elements from TNaF, but is not meant to immediately come off as one.

  • How big will the game be?

    A: The game be very large by any means, however there will be some different paths/endings to seek along with secrets and extra modes for replay-ability value.
